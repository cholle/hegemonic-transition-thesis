--- (Synthesized in Framework Chats)

**"Rent-Avoidance Theory of Paradigm Transition"** *(Analytical Frameworks chat)*
> Subordinate actors build alternatives not from strategic vision but from structural necessity. China entered EVs because Western patent architecture made ICE prohibitively expensive — the open field drove the move. US hegemonic institutions have shifted from productive coordination to rent-extraction, systematically compelling rivals to build around them.

**"Creative Obstruction"** *(Analytical Frameworks chat)*
> American capitalism has shifted from Schumpeterian creative destruction to creative obstruction — actively blocking new productive combinations to protect incumbent rent streams.

**"AI breaks the Perez model"** *(Calleo chat / AI-Perez chat)*
> AI is the first technology that replaces the primary input of *all* prior paradigms — human cognitive labor. If deployment compresses rather than expands the wage base, the institutional conditions for a Perezian golden age cannot obtain. The frameworks haven't yet addressed this.

**"Solar Theology Thesis"**
> Distributed solar energy is structurally un-monopolizable; its physical resource base (sunlight) makes every downstream rent-extraction point permanently vulnerable to diversification pressure — materially incompatible with hegemonic concentration.

**"Counter-Surveillance Adequacy"**
> Breaking adversary confidence rather than matching AI surveillance capability; institutional incoherence as AI-era stealth.

**"Fourth Monetary Formula"** *(Calleo summary chat, March 2026)*
> The Trump-era weaponization of the dollar system (sanctions, SWIFT exclusion, asset freezes) combined with direct military asset-stripping (Venezuela oil, Iran regime change) represents a fourth formula following Calleo's three (Bretton Woods, Nixon, Reagan). Each formula is more aggressive, more transparent, and provokes harder resistance. The fourth drops the pretense of systemic management entirely — Hudson's protection racket without residual legitimation.
