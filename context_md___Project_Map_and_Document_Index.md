# context.md — Hegemonic Transition Thesis Project

## What This Project Is

A long-form analytical thesis synthesizing eleven theorists into an integrated framework for understanding hegemonic decline, systemic transition, and technological paradigm change. Applied to live geopolitical events (Venezuela, Iran) as primary case studies. The thesis argues that US hegemonic institutions have shifted from productive coordination to rent extraction, and that this coercion structurally compels subordinate actors to build alternatives — producing the institutional skeleton of a successor order not through strategic vision but through the logic of rent avoidance.

**Working title:** *Hegemonic Rent-Extraction and the Structural Logic of Paradigm Transition*

**Assessment:** Between ambitious master's thesis and PhD proposal. Material is "notebook-shaped" — the remaining work is primarily organizational rather than intellectual.

## Original Theoretical Contributions

These emerged from the synthesis and do not exist in any single source:

1. **Rent-avoidance theory of paradigm transition** — Subordinate actors build alternatives from structural necessity, not strategic vision
2. **Creative obstruction** — Active use of state power to prevent new productive combinations (inverts Schumpeter)
3. **Solar theology thesis** — Distributed solar is structurally un-monopolizable; materially incompatible with hegemonic concentration
4. **The fourth monetary formula** — Trump-era weaponization of dollar system + military asset-stripping extends Calleo's three-formula sequence
5. **Counter-surveillance adequacy** — Breaking adversary confidence rather than matching capability
6. **The simulacrum-capacity ratchet** — Gap between projected and actual power widens irreversibly; has a social drive mechanism (Turchin)
7. **"AI breaks the Perez model"** — AI is the first paradigm that compresses rather than expands the wage base, foreclosing the golden age

## Three Scenarios Tracked

1. **China-centered multipolar trade order** — Arrighi's predicted endpoint; complicated by Olson (free-rider) and Acharya (may not center)
2. **America as New Rome** — Domination without hegemony; asset-stripping; overstretch risk; AI panopticon as enabler
3. **Systemic chaos / turbulence** — Assessed as most likely current phase; may be semi-permanent (Acharya's multiplex as destination, not passage)
