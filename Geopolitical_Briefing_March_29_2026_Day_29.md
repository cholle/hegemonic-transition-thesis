## LEAD STORY: HOUTHIS ENTER THE WAR (DAY 28)

On March 28, Yemen's Houthi movement fired a barrage of ballistic missiles at southern Israel — its first attack since the Gaza ceasefire last October. The IDF intercepted the missiles. Houthi military spokesman Yahya Saree vowed continued operations.

**What changed:** For 28 days the Houthis were the last major Axis of Resistance holdout. Hezbollah and Iraqi militias joined within days of the Feb 28 strikes. The Houthis waited, citing the 2025 ceasefire constraint and apparent expectation of an Iranian signal.

**Why now:** The Houthis announced three "red lines" on March 27 — entry of new US alliances, use of Red Sea for US/Israeli attacks on Iran, and continued US-Israeli escalation. The April 6 deadline and ongoing strikes apparently crossed one of those lines.

**The critical variable that did NOT activate:** Red Sea shipping attacks and Bab al-Mandeb closure remain unexecuted. The Houthis are striking Israel — not tankers. The "nuclear option" of dual-chokepoint closure has not been used. This is a deliberately measured escalation, not the maximum response.

**Framework read:** Olson free-rider dynamics have inverted inside the Axis of Resistance. The Houthis sat out while Iran absorbed punishment — a rational calculation. Entry now, at a moment of US-Iran ceasefire talks and an imminent deadline, suggests either (a) Iranian signal that talks are failing and escalation is preferable, or (b) autonomous Houthi decision that the ceasefire constraint no longer holds. Either way: the structural constraint that held the dual-chokepoint off-table for 28 days has broken.

Iranian ghost fleet continues exporting — 19 tracked loadings since Feb 28, ~28 million barrels, ~$2 billion in revenue for the IRGC.

The March 27 parliamentary sovereignty legislation provided the legal architecture; the toll booth is the operational architecture. Acharya's multiplex world is characterized by overlapping nodes of functional authority — Iran is constructing one in real time, under bombardment, at the world's most consequential chokepoint.

**Framework read (Hudson):** Iran is running a protection racket. The irony is structural: the US protection racket (pay for security, accept dollar hegemony) is being mirrored by its adversary at the exact node the US went to war to control. The yuan denomination is not incidental — it is the rent-avoidance infrastructure made explicit.

**The non-transferability thesis under live test:** US bombardment has not transferred Hormuz control to the US. Iran administers it from under the rubble. The productive infrastructure being built — tolling apparatus, legal sovereignty claim, yuan denomination — cannot be transferred through conquest without Iran's cooperation in dismantling it.

## STORY 3: TRUMP'S 15-POINT PLAN REJECTED — APRIL 6 DEADLINE

1. Halt all US-Israeli "aggression and assassinations"
2. Mechanisms guaranteeing the war does not resume
3. Payment of war damages and reparations
4. End attacks on Hezbollah (Lebanon) and pro-Iranian militias (Iraq)

**The narrative gap is now structurally confirmed:** US says talks are productive and Iran wants a deal; Iran says there are no talks and no ceasefire is sought. **Condition 5 is analytically decisive:** Iran is demanding that ending the war requires international recognition of its Hormuz authority — converting what began as a military blockade into a permanent legal entitlement. This is a non-starter for the US. It means Iran's stated peace terms structurally preclude the US achieving its stated war aims. The endgame contradiction identified at Day 13 is now explicit and formally articulated.

**Pakistan as mediator:** Pakistan's PM Sharif confirmed indirect talks, with Turkey and Egypt also "passing messages." Larijani is still dead. The architecture of a functional deal corridor does not exist.

Brent at $112 is serious but the IEA threshold of "total market failure" has not been reached. Kindleberger's cascading failure prediction is being absorbed by institutional density — a point in Acharya's favor.

3. **The dual simulacrum cuts both ways.** If Iran is also performing "no talks" for domestic audiences while secretly negotiating, the thesis's emphasis on Iran as rational rent-avoider becomes complicated. The 5 counter-conditions could be an opening bid, not a final position. Hold this open.
