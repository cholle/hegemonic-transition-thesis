## Hudson's Position

### Core Framing
Hudson reads Epic Fury as the structural terminus of US oil-control strategy — not an aberration but the logical endpoint of the protection racket's terminal phase. Key claims:

**Negotiations as cover, war as policy:** The Oman diplomatic corridor (Feb 25–28) was live and progressing. Busaidi confirmed "active and serious negotiations." Hudson's reading: the attack was launched *precisely because* negotiations were succeeding. A deal that normalized Iranian oil exports on non-dollar terms would have accelerated petrodollar exit. The war aim was not to prevent a nuclear program — it was to prevent a settlement that would have formalized dollar-bypass infrastructure.

**Deliberate attrition strategy:** Iran is not losing. The missile campaign is calibrated to exhaust US interceptor stocks while preserving Iranian strategic reserves. Hudson reads this as rational economic warfare — the cost asymmetry is the weapon, not the missiles themselves.

**Dollar-oil nexus under terminal pressure:** Iranian dedollarization demands (oil in yuan, euro, bilateral currencies) represent the specific mechanism Hudson has tracked since *Super Imperialism*. The war is the protection racket enforcing dollar invoicing through military coercion when economic incentives failed.

**"Last chance" framing:** Hudson has used terminal-urgency language — this is not hegemonic confidence but a rearguard action. The hegemon attacks because it is losing structurally, not because it is winning.

### Thesis Mapping
Hudson provides the **structural-determinist layer**: the war was coming because the protection racket requires enforcement, the petrodollar requires oil invoicing, and Iran represented the most advanced dollar-bypass architecture. Calleo's predatory hegemon explains *why* this was coming; Hudson explains *what specifically* triggered the timing.

**Strength:** The Venezuela-Iran paired case perfectly confirms Hudson's terminal sequence (military seizure → normalization → corporate extraction). Venezuela succeeded; Iran is the resistant variant.

**Limitation:** Hudson's structural determinism underspecifies the agential mechanism. *Why this form, this timing, this target sequence?* Mearsheimer fills this gap.

## Mearsheimer's Position

### Core Framing
Mearsheimer reads Epic Fury as an agential trap — contingent, avoidable, and driven by lobby capture. Key claims:

**Netanyahu successfully manipulated Trump:** Every prior president (Obama, Bush, even Trump in his first term) refused the war Netanyahu wanted. This time, the combination of the Oct 7 political debt, MAGA evangelical-Zionist alignment, and the intelligence handover (Khamenei location) created conditions Netanyahu had never previously achieved.

**The commitment device:** By handing Washington targeting intelligence on Khamenei's location, Israel created a fait accompli — the decision was converted from "whether to strike" into "the strike is already in motion; stand with Israel or against it." Mearsheimer identifies this as the specific mechanism of US entry. (The thesis calls this the *Israel Commitment Device* — Notebook entry under cross-framework connections.)

**Decapitation-regime-change failure:** Netanyahu sold a scenario — strike the leadership, the regime collapses, install a compliant successor — that has now failed. The succession produced Mojtaba under IRGC pressure, the worst possible outcome for US deal-seeking. There is no plausible off-ramp.

**No viable exit:** Mearsheimer is explicit that the US has no good options. The war aims are either already achieved (and therefore the war should end, but Israel won't let it) or impossible to achieve (and therefore continuing is irrational). The coalition is trapped.

### Thesis Mapping
Mearsheimer provides the **contingency/mechanism layer**: this specific war in this specific form resulted from the Israel commitment device, the wrong payoff function, and Netanyahu's unique political circumstances. Without these agential factors, the structural pressures Hudson identifies might have produced a different form of conflict or a delayed timeline.

**Core tension with Hudson:** Hudson: the war was structurally determined. Mearsheimer: it was contingent on Netanyahu's political survival calculus and Trump's specific vulnerabilities. The thesis position: **Calleo explains why a conflict was coming structurally; Mearsheimer explains why it happened in this form now.** Both needed; neither sufficient.

**Strength:** The commitment device analysis is empirically confirmed by the Kent resignation — Kent stated publicly that Iran posed no imminent threat, that key decision-makers were excluded from briefing the president, and that Israel drove the decision. This is the strongest single-source confirmation of the agential-trap mechanism.

**Limitation:** Mearsheimer's lobby-capture framing underspecifies the mechanism. The thesis's *wrong payoff function* concept is more precise — it's not that Netanyahu captured US policy through influence, but that Israel's payoff function (eschatological/existential) is structurally incompatible with US's preferred extractive outcome, and the commitment device locked the US into a coalition it cannot govern.

## Thesis Falsification Checks

**What would falsify Hudson here:** A negotiated settlement that preserved dollar invoicing for Iranian oil while ending the conflict. This would show the war was about nuclear weapons, not dollar hegemony. Currently: no evidence pointing this direction.

**What would falsify Mearsheimer here:** Evidence that the war would have occurred without Israeli pressure — i.e., that the Trump administration had independently developed war aims and timing. Kent resignation is the strongest counter-evidence to this falsification.

**What both readings share:** Neither Hudson nor Mearsheimer disputes that the US has no viable endgame. This convergence across structuralist and realist frameworks is itself analytically significant — the thesis's S3 (chaos) dominance is supported from both directions.
