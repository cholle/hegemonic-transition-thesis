# The Simulacrum-Capacity Ratchet

## A Mechanism of Terminal Hegemonic Decline

---

### I. The Mechanism Stated

Declining hegemonic power generates a self-accelerating feedback loop in the military-prestige domain: as material capacity declines, the cost of *simulating* non-decline increases, because the gap between sign and referent that the simulation must cover widens. The increased simulation cost drains resources from the productive and military base the simulation is meant to represent, which widens the gap further, which raises simulation costs again. This is the **simulacrum-capacity ratchet** — a mechanism operative in the terminal phase of hegemonic decline that is structurally distinct from, though interacting with, Arrighi's finance/production split and Calleo's predatory rent extraction.

The mechanism was identified empirically by Malcolm Kyeyune in analysis of Operation Epic Fury (March 2026): the US Navy, reduced from 600 to fewer than 300 ships, cannot reduce its presence mission tempo without *appearing* weaker, because the appearance of weakness triggers the cascade the appearance is meant to prevent. The logical response — scale presence missions to actual capacity — is politically foreclosed. The result is sailors working double cycles, equipment run past maintenance thresholds, readiness rates declining, and the Gerald Ford carrier deployed on a ninth month of operations with non-functional sanitation systems. The simulation of capacity accelerates the material decline it conceals.

---

### II. The Snowball Inversion

The structural logic of the ratchet becomes clearest when read against its precise inverse: Terutomo Ozawa's account of the East Asian productive snowball, synthesized by Arrighi in *Adam Smith in Beijing*. Ozawa describes a self-reinforcing ascending process: productive capacity generates conditions for further productive capacity accumulation. Each increment reduces the cost of the next — falling unit costs, technology transfer depth, labor skill accumulation, infrastructure learning curves. The flywheel is self-reinforcing on the way up.

The simulacrum-capacity ratchet is the exact structural inversion of the Ozawa snowball, operating on the descending arc of the same systemic cycle. Where the snowball is a *material* process of compounding real productive capacity, the ratchet is a *semiotic* process of compounding simulation cost. The US is no longer producing military power as its primary output; it is producing the *sign* of military power at ever-increasing cost per unit of sign produced. The product has fully inverted.

This inversion is not coincidental. The two dynamics are temporally synchronized at the transition point rather than sequential: as East Asian productive capacity compounds (Ozawa snowball ascending), it directly undermines the material base the American simulacrum is trying to represent, which increases the cost of the simulation, which accelerates the drain on the remaining material base. The Chinese EV/solar/semiconductor snowball does not merely displace US productive capacity — it makes the US's *performance* of capacity more expensive per unit of credibility purchased. The ascending and descending arcs are causally linked, not merely parallel.

### III. Baudrillard's Stages and the Punctuation Problem

The ratchet operates within a broader semiotic trajectory that Baudrillard's stages of the image illuminate. Military capacity, in the hegemonic cycle, passes through: an image that reflects reality (post-WWII US genuinely supreme), then an image that *masks* degradation of reality (Cold War parity concealed by spending), then an image that masks the *absence* of the capacity it purports to represent (the current phase — simulacrum as active concealment), then pure self-referential performance with no material anchor (stage four — irrelevance).

Stage three — masking absence — is the most *expensive* phase. It requires active effort precisely because the gap is maximum while still requiring concealment. Stage four costs nothing; it is simply ignored. The ratchet therefore reaches peak velocity in stage three and terminates when the simulation shatters — not gradually degrades, but collapses punctuatedly when the gap between sign and referent becomes publicly indefensible.

This punctuation structure is the specific contribution of the ratchet mechanism to Arrighi's chaos phase. Arrighi identifies the chaos phase as a period of systemic turbulence and indeterminate duration. The ratchet explains the *internal trigger structure* within that phase: the hologram does not fade, it breaks. Operation Epic Fury appears to be operating as exactly this kind of trigger event — Chinese commercial satellite imagery of bombed-out Gulf bases circulating on X, the Wall Street Journal embedding 11-interceptors-for-2-missiles footage in its own Twitter thread, CNN showing F-15s falling while narrating over the footage. The information environment that stage-three concealment required has already fractured past the threshold of recovery.

### IV. The Social Base of the Lock-In (Turchin)

The ratchet is not merely structural — it has an active social constituency that forecloses the only rational exit. Managed strategic withdrawal with controlled image management would arrest the ratchet by accepting a bounded loss of prestige in exchange for resource reallocation to the material base. This exit is politically unavailable.

Turchin's elite overproduction framework explains why. The military brass, defense contractor complex, think-tank infrastructure, and the political class whose positional value is constituted by imperial prestige are surplus elites whose entire reproduction depends on the simulacrum's maintenance. Acknowledging decline destroys their positional value simultaneously and completely — there is no partial acknowledgment available. They will therefore escalate simulation costs at each choice point rather than accept any acknowledgment of the gap. The ratchet has a social drive mechanism, not merely a structural one.

The post-defeat political dynamic Kyeyune identifies follows the same logic: Trump as scapegoat allows the hegemonic self-image to survive the material defeat by attributing collapse to individual failure rather than structural decline. This is the East German question — "who are we if communism is over?" — answered by refusing to answer it. The scapegoating narrative extends the simulacrum's political lifespan at the cost of precluding the institutional adjustment that could rebuild the material base. The ratchet continues under new management.

### V. The AI Question: Does New Technology Lower Hologram Cost?

The simulacrum-capacity ratchet intersects with AI and the datacenter build-out in ways that initially appear to offer an escape from the mechanism but, on examination, deepen it.

AI lowers hologram cost in the *narrative* register: synthetic media, influence operations, and targeted information warfare reduce the marginal cost of producing signs of power per unit of narrative output. This is the domain where the US retains genuine competitive advantage and where AI investment is most directly applicable to simulation cost reduction.

However, the ratchet is currently operating in the *material* register. Broken toilets, interceptor depletion, 50 bombers substituting for 12,000, presence missions overextending 300 ships designed for 600 — these are not narrative failures. They are material legibility failures, and they are being exposed by commercial satellite transparency and social media circulation that operates independently of US narrative capacity. AI narrative capability is lowering the cost of producing the wrong product: the gap is in the material register, and no amount of narrative optimization closes it.

In three additional registers, AI raises rather than lowers hologram cost:

**Capital cannibalization.** Stargate-scale datacenter investment and chip fab subsidization represent enormous capital allocated to sign-production infrastructure rather than the productive/military capacity the signs are meant to represent. This is Arrighi's terminal financialization in a new substrate: AI valuation performing as the store of future productive capacity while the industrial base goes unrenewed. The datacenter is the new hologram investment — it deepens the ratchet rather than escaping it.

**Symmetric transparency acceleration.** Adversaries receive AI targeting and analysis capability on open-source timelines. Iranian hacking operations, algorithmically coordinated drone swarm sequencing, and Chinese AI-enhanced satellite analysis all improve adversary capacity to expose the material gap the US is trying to conceal. The force multiplier multiplies on both sides, and the marginal effect on hologram cost is negative for the incumbent: better adversary targeting produces more undeniable material losses, more satellite imagery, faster hologram collapse.

**The AI rent point is structurally vulnerable.** US strategy attempts to migrate hegemonic rent extraction from petrodollar chokepoints to cognitive/AI chokepoints — chip export controls, TSMC dependency, frontier model monopoly. But the solar theology thesis applies directly: the underlying commodities (mathematics, silicon, electricity, training data) are not physically monopolizable, and DeepSeek demonstrated near-frontier capability at a fraction of expected cost, showing the arbitrage pressure already operating. The US is attempting to build a new rent point on the same structurally vulnerable foundation as the old ones.

**The one genuine exception** is the AI-as-Auftragstaktik hypothesis: if AI-assisted command and coordination could give US commanders the operational picture that enables mission-oriented tactics — the "whole plan in their head" that Kyeyune identifies as the Iranian advantage — that would represent real hologram-cost reduction by actually rebuilding strategic coherence rather than simulating it. This is structurally distinct from narrative AI and represents a genuine military-AI pathway. However, the institutional barrier to Auftragstaktik is not informational — it is political economy at two levels. First, US ends are undefined not because commanders lack information but because the political system cannot define them without triggering the domestic collapse the hologram is meant to prevent. AI cannot solve an ends-definition problem. Second, and more structurally: the simulacrum itself requires centralized narrative control. Headquarters must continuously "see" and "narrate" the battlefield for domestic political consumption — this is the mechanism by which military action is converted into sign-value for the political class. Genuinely decentralized, autonomous mission tactics is *incompatible* with this requirement. The simulation needs centralized oversight; Auftragstaktik requires the opposite. AI-as-Auftragstaktik is therefore not merely politically blocked — it is structurally foreclosed by the ratchet's own operating requirements. Implementing it would mean dismantling the apparatus the simulation depends on.

### VI. Placement in the Overall Framework

The simulacrum-capacity ratchet operates as a *specific mechanism* within Arrighi's terminal hegemonic phase, not a replacement for it. Its relationship to the other framework components:

- **Arrighi**: the ratchet is the military-prestige parallel to the finance/production split. Both are symptoms of the same terminal phase; they interact (financialization hollows the productive base military capacity depends on; the ratchet drains the remaining resources that could theoretically rebuild it) but are not identical.
- **Calleo**: Calleo's predatory rent extraction operates on the economic architecture; the ratchet operates on the military-prestige architecture. Both are self-defeating — Calleo's coercive occupation of rent points accelerates rent-avoidance; the ratchet's simulation expenditure accelerates material decline. They are parallel mechanisms in different registers, jointly characterizing the terminal phase.
- **Ozawa/Arrighi (snowball)**: the ratchet is the structural inverse of the productive snowball, temporally synchronized with it at the transition point, causally linked through the mechanism by which ascending productive capacity raises the cost of incumbent simulation.
- **Turchin**: the social base of the ratchet lock-in. Elite overproduction explains why the rational exit (managed withdrawal) is politically unavailable.
- **Perez**: the ratchet is downstream of the installation-without-deployment failure. The hollowed productive base that makes the simulation necessary was produced by financialization's foreclosure of the productive deployment phase.
- **Rent-avoidance theory**: the ratchet extends rent-avoidance logic into the military-prestige domain. The coercive pressure (decapitation doctrine, presence missions as deterrence) selects for more distributed, harder-to-destroy adversary organizational forms (Iranian mosaic warfare as Auftragstaktik), making the enforcement mechanism self-defeating at the organizational level as well as the economic one.
